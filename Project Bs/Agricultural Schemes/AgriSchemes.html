Agricultural Schemes

Agricultural Schemes Description Government websites
Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maandhan yojana Under PM Kisan Maandhan scheme about 5 crore marginalised farmers will get a minimum pension of Rs 3000 per month on attaining the age of 60. Click Here!
Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) This is an actuarial premium based scheme where farmer has to pay maximum premium of 2 percent for Kharif, 1.5 percent for Rabi food & oilseed crops and 5 percent for annual commercial or horticultural crops and the remaining part of the actuarial or bidded premium is equally shared by the Central & State Government. Click Here!
Kisan Credit Card (KCC) scheme Under this scheme, farmers are given short-term loans to purchase equipment & for their other expenses as well. There are many banks that offer KCC including SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis. Click Here!
Soil Health Card Scheme The Soil Health Cards gives information to farmers on nutrient status of their soil along with recommendation on appropriate dosage of nutrients to be applied for improving soil health and its fertility. Click Here!
Livestock insurance Scheme Livestock insurance Scheme is aimed at providing protection mechanism to farmers as well as cattle rearers against any eventual loss of animals because of death Click Here!
Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY) PMKSY focuses on creating sources for assured irrigation, also creating protective irrigation by harnessing rain water at micro level through ‘Jal Sanchay’ & ‘Jal Sinchan’. Click Here!
Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) According to the scheme, farmers will be encouraged to form groups or clusters and take to organic farming methods over large areas in the country. The aim is to form 10,000 clusters over the next three years and bring about five lakh acres of agricultural area under organic farming. Click Here!
